BMW Maintenance Guide

Keep Your BMW Running Smoothly with Our Maintenance Guide Regular maintenance is crucial to keeping your BMW performing at its best. Routine care helps ensure your car runs efficiently, lasts longer, and maintains its safety and reliability. Key tasks like oil changes, brake inspections, tire rotations, and fluid checks are essential for optimal performance. By sticking to a regular maintenance schedule, you prevent costly repairs, improve fuel efficiency, and maintain the car’s resale value. This guide provides a clear roadmap for what to check and when to service your BMW, ensuring it stays in peak condition for years.

Maintenance Schedule

Follow this schedule to ensure your BMW stays in top condition:

Interval Task
Every 10,000 km (6,000 miles) Oil and Filter Change
Every 20,000 km (12,000 miles) Air Filter Replacement
Every 40,000 km (24,000 miles) Spark Plug Replacement
Every 60,000 km (36,000 miles) Brake Fluid Change
Every 100,000 km (62,000 miles) Coolant Flush
Every 120,000 km (75,000 miles) Transmission Fluid Change

Maintenance Tips

  • Regular Fluid Checks: Always ensure your engine oil, brake fluid, and coolant levels are topped up.
  • Inspect Tires: Check tire pressure regularly and replace tires when tread is low.
  • Keep an Eye on Brakes: Listen for any unusual sounds and get your brake pads checked when needed.
  • Battery Care: Inspect your battery regularly, especially before winter, to ensure it’s in good condition.
  • Keep It Clean: Regularly wash and wax your BMW to protect the paintwork and prevent rust.

When to Seek Professional Service

If you notice any of the following signs, it’s time to take your BMW to a certified technician:

  • Unusual engine noises or vibrations.
  • Check engine light comes on.
  • Difficulty starting the car or rough idling.
  • Excessive smoke from the exhaust.
  • Changes in handling or unusual sounds when braking.
