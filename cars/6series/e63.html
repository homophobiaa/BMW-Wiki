BMW E63

The second-generation 6 Series Coupe (2003 - 2010)

Car Specifications

  • Engine Options: Inline-6, V8, V10 (M6)
  • Power: 325 - 507 hp
  • Torque: 450 - 520 Nm
  • Transmission: 6-speed manual, 6-speed automatic, 7-speed SMG
  • Top Speed: 250 km/h (155 mph) for the M6
  • 0-100 km/h: 4.6 seconds (M6)
  • Fuel Economy: 9.2 - 14.5 L/100 km (25 - 16 mpg)
  • Production Years: 2003 - 2010
  • Body Style: Coupe, Convertible
  • Drive Type: Rear-Wheel Drive, All-Wheel Drive (xDrive models)
  • Weight: 1,600 - 1,800 kg

About the BMW E63

The BMW E63 is the second-generation 6 Series, first introduced in 2003. Known for its striking and aggressive design, the E63 combined performance with luxury, making it one of BMW’s most iconic grand tourers.

The M6 model, powered by a 5.0L V10 engine, showcased the ultimate performance in the lineup. The E63’s sharp lines and powerful engine choices helped solidify its reputation as a true sports coupe.

Key Features

  • Performance: M6 model powered by a 5.0L V10 engine producing 507 hp.
  • Design: Aggressive and athletic styling with a coupe’s elegance and luxury.
  • Technology: Adaptive suspension, premium sound system, and iDrive system.

Technical Information

The BMW E63 offered a range of performance engines, including the high-performance M6 with the S85 V10:

Engine Codes and Iterations

  • N54: 3.0L Inline-6, used in 630i, 635i.
  • N62: 4.8L V8, used in 645i, 650i.
  • S85: 5.0L V10, used in the M6, producing 507 hp.

Conclusion

The BMW E63 remains one of the most thrilling coupes in BMW’s history. Its combination of aggressive styling, luxury features, and exceptional performance – particularly in the M6 – ensures its status as a modern classic.
