BMW X3

The Luxury Compact SUV (2003 - Present)

Car Specifications

  • Engine Options: Inline-4, Inline-6, Diesel options
  • Power: 184 - 503 hp (M version)
  • Torque: 400 - 650 Nm
  • Transmission: 8-speed automatic, 6-speed manual
  • Top Speed: 250 km/h (155 mph) for M40i
  • 0-100 km/h: 4.8 seconds (X3 M)
  • Fuel Economy: 7.0 - 10.5 L/100 km (33 - 22 mpg)
  • Production Years: 2003 - Present
  • Body Style: SUV, 5-door
  • Drive Type: All-Wheel Drive (xDrive)
  • Weight: 1,700 - 2,100 kg

About the BMW X3

The BMW X3 is BMW’s luxury compact SUV, offering exceptional driving dynamics and a versatile, luxurious interior. It has been a popular choice for drivers who want the sporty feel of a BMW with the practicality of an SUV. Introduced in 2003, the X3 has seen several updates to improve performance, comfort, and technology.

With its combination of powerful engine options, smart technology, and impressive performance, the BMW X3 stands as one of the best-selling BMW models worldwide.

Key Features

  • Performance: M version with up to 503 hp for thrilling driving experience.
  • Technology: Advanced iDrive system, optional gesture controls, and driving assistance features.
  • Comfort: Spacious cabin, heated seats, premium materials, and an optional panoramic sunroof.

Technical Information

The BMW X3 offers a range of engine configurations and technical advancements:

Engine Codes and Iterations

  • B48: 2.0L Inline-4, used in the X3 xDrive20i and 30i.
  • B57: 2.0L Diesel Inline-4, used in the X3 20d and 30d.
  • S58: 3.0L Inline-6 Turbo, used in the X3 M and X3 M40i.
  • B58: 2.0L Inline-4 Turbo, used in the X3 xDrive30i.

Conclusion

The BMW X3 is an outstanding luxury SUV that combines BMW’s signature driving performance with the practicality and comfort expected from a premium compact SUV. Whether you’re looking for a high-performance model like the X3 M or a more fuel-efficient option, the X3 offers a range of configurations to suit every driver’s needs.
