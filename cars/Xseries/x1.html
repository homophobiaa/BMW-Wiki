BMW X1

The Compact Luxury SUV (2009 - Present)

Car Specifications

  • Engine Options: Inline-3, Inline-4, Diesel options
  • Power: 136 - 306 hp
  • Torque: 220 - 450 Nm
  • Transmission: 6-speed manual, 7-speed automatic
  • Top Speed: 250 km/h (155 mph) for M35i
  • 0-100 km/h: 5.4 seconds (M35i)
  • Fuel Economy: 5.0 - 8.0 L/100 km (47 - 29 mpg)
  • Production Years: 2009 - Present
  • Body Style: SUV, 5-door
  • Drive Type: Front-Wheel Drive, All-Wheel Drive (xDrive models)
  • Weight: 1,400 - 1,700 kg

About the BMW X1

The BMW X1 is BMW's entry-level compact luxury SUV, offering a blend of performance, practicality, and luxury. First introduced in 2009, the X1 has become one of the most popular models in the BMW lineup, especially in urban markets.

The X1 combines the driving dynamics that BMW is known for with a versatile, spacious interior and an array of high-tech features. Available with a range of petrol, diesel, and hybrid engines, the X1 is a flexible choice for drivers seeking a compact, efficient, and premium vehicle.

Key Features

  • Performance: M35i model with 306 hp and 450 Nm of torque for dynamic performance.
  • Technology: Includes BMW's iDrive infotainment system, driver-assistance features, and optional adaptive suspension.
  • Practicality: Compact size with a spacious cabin and generous cargo space, perfect for urban driving.

Technical Information

The BMW X1 comes with a variety of engine options to suit different driving preferences:

Engine Codes and Iterations

  • B38: 1.5L Inline-3, used in X1 sDrive18i.
  • B48: 2.0L Inline-4, used in X1 xDrive20i and 25i models.
  • B47: 2.0L Diesel Inline-4, used in 20d and 25d models.
  • B46: 2.0L Hybrid, used in plug-in hybrid X1 models.

Conclusion

The BMW X1 offers the perfect blend of BMW's luxury and performance in a compact SUV. With its efficient engines, smart design, and premium features, the X1 is an excellent choice for urban drivers looking for an agile and versatile vehicle.
