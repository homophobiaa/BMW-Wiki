BMW F10

The seventh-generation 5 Series (2010 - 2017)

Car Specifications

  • Engine Options: Inline-4, Inline-6, V8, V8 Hybrid, I6 Diesel (F10 520d)
  • Power: 184 - 600 hp (M5)
  • Torque: 290 - 700 Nm (M5)
  • Transmission: 6-speed manual, 8-speed automatic, 7-speed dual-clutch (M5)
  • Top Speed: 250 km/h (155 mph) for the M5
  • 0-100 km/h: 3.9 seconds (M5)
  • Fuel Economy: 6.0 - 14.0 L/100 km (47 - 16 mpg)
  • Production Years: 2010 - 2017
  • Body Style: Sedan, Touring (Wagon)
  • Drive Type: Rear-Wheel Drive, All-Wheel Drive (xDrive models)
  • Weight: 1,500 - 2,000 kg

About the BMW F10

The BMW F10 debuted as the seventh-generation of the 5 Series with a refined, elegant design that maintained the sporty, luxury appeal of its predecessor. Featuring cutting-edge technology like the iDrive system with a larger display, the F10 offers a great blend of performance and luxury.

The F10 M5 model, powered by a 4.4L V8 twin-turbo engine, represents the pinnacle of the series with astonishing performance and luxury features.

Image Gallery

Key Features

  • Performance: M5 model powered by a 4.4L V8 twin-turbo delivering 600 hp.
  • Design: A sleek and sophisticated design with a focus on aerodynamics and luxury.
  • Technology: Advanced iDrive system, lane departure warning, and adaptive cruise control.

Technical Information

The BMW F10 features a variety of advanced engines and configurations:

Engine Codes and Iterations

  • N20: Inline-4, used in 520i, 528i.
  • N55: Inline-6, used in 530i, 535i, 540i.
  • S63: 4.4L V8 twin-turbo, used in M5, producing 600 hp.
  • M57: Diesel inline-6, used in 525d, 530d.

Conclusion

The BMW F10 marks a significant evolution in the 5 Series lineage, offering greater performance, efficiency, and advanced technology. The F10 M5, with its powerhouse engine, remains a standout for driving enthusiasts.
